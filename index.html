Our Cheese

Made by Integritiy, Quality and All Love.
Little Hereford
Full flavoured hard cheese matured for 4- months minimum.The flavour is half way between a Caerphilly and a Cheddar, full, rounded, creamy, getting sweeter and more tangy with age.
Hereford Sage
Made with the Little Hereford curd and flavoured with fresh sage this cheese is pressed for 24 hours and then matured for between 6-10 weeks. Sage is a very traditional addition to cheese and produces a nice fresh flavour without over powering the cheese itself.
Monkland
Shaped in a colander, so popular with many a cheesemaker, the curds are drained and turned and the resulting form is brine bathed overnight and then matured for between 4-8 weeks to produce a hard cheese with a light, crumbly texture and creamy, yet acidic (yes this is good) flavour. And such fun to watch being made.
Monkland with Garlic and Chives
The garlic Monkland is a drained curd cheese like the plain Monkland. We experimented with flavours for many years and of all the variations we played with this was the most popular. Not necessarily for the purists. Unashamedly garlicky and at its best a lovely crumbly texture and nice acidic bite.
Smoke Little Hereford
The Little Hereford is matured for six months before being delivered to the Black Mountain smokery in Crickhowell where it is expertly smoked for 24 hours over oak chips.
Blue Monk Half
These are soft, creamy, blues made individually at approx 250g. They are pierced twice and turned as they mature for up to 28 days before wrapping.They then continue to mature and soften as they age becoming softer and creamier with time.
